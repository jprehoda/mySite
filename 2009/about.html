My design career started back in 2001 in the pagination department at Tribune Media services.
This is where I was introduced to design software. Having been comfortable with computers all
my life, picking up something new like PhotoShop or Quark Xpress was only second nature.

From there, I began exploring positions within the print industry. My position at the Post-Star
allowed me to see the backend of print by working in close proximity to the printing press. I
then moved back to Tribune where I picked up a more challenging role as a design
consultant. I gained much needed experience in customer service and delegation
while perfomring my duties as a design consultant.

It wasn't until 2007 when I began to hone my design skills. I was challenged on a
whole new level as an electronic ad designer at The Naples Daily News. I
quickly became comfortable in my new role and the creativity began to flow.

My current position as a website architect focuses mainly on creating table-less
websites. But many other skills are called in to play from day to day. These skills
include the use of various APIs, jQuery and Django among many others.

I'm always on the look out for ways to improve user interactions with the sites I
maintain. I'd love to see what challenges you can offer me. Please feel free to
view my resume or contact me.
